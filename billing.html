Billing & Insurance Information

Last Updated: July 17, 2025

At Madina Community Hospital & Specialist Services, we are committed to providing transparent and accessible healthcare. Understanding your billing and insurance options is an important part of your healthcare journey. This page provides detailed information to help you navigate financial aspects of your care.

Accepted Insurance Providers

We work with a wide range of insurance providers to ensure that quality healthcare is accessible to as many patients as possible. Please note that it is always advisable to confirm your coverage directly with your insurance provider prior to your visit.

Private Insurance

We accept most major private health insurance plans. Some of our commonly accepted private insurance partners include:

  • Acme Health Insurance
  • GlobalCare Medical
  • Pinnacle Health Solutions
  • Unity Life & Health
  • (And many more - please contact us to verify your specific plan)

National Health Insurance Scheme (NHIS)

Madina Community Hospital & Specialist Services is a proud partner of the National Health Insurance Scheme (NHIS). We are dedicated to serving NHIS cardholders and ensuring they receive the care they need under the scheme's provisions.

  • Please ensure your NHIS card is active and presented at the time of registration.
  • Certain services or medications may not be fully covered by NHIS; our staff will inform you of any potential out-of-pocket expenses.

Understanding Your Bill and Billing Procedures

We strive to make our billing process as clear as possible. Your bill will reflect the services you received, including consultations, laboratory tests, procedures, and medications. Here's what you need to know:

  • Co-pays and Deductibles: Depending on your insurance plan, you may be responsible for a co-payment (a fixed amount paid at the time of service) or a deductible (the amount you must pay before your insurance begins to cover costs).
  • Out-of-Pocket Expenses: For services not covered by insurance, or if you are a cash-and-carry patient, you will be responsible for the full cost of services. We accept various payment methods, including cash, mobile money, and major credit/debit cards.
  • Itemized Bills: You can request an itemized bill at any time if you need a detailed breakdown of charges.
  • Pre-authorization: Some services or procedures may require pre-authorization from your insurance company. Our team can assist you with this process.

Financial Assistance & Payment Plans

We understand that healthcare costs can sometimes be a burden. If you are facing financial difficulties, please do not hesitate to speak with our billing department. We may be able to discuss flexible payment plans or guide you through options for financial assistance.

Our goal is to ensure that financial concerns do not prevent you from receiving necessary medical care.
